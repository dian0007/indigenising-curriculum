Purpose and Scope

This section defines the aim of Indigenising La Trobe’s curriculum, framing it as a commitment to embed Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander knowledges, cultures, and perspectives across all subjects.

References: Best (2017), Williams (1998)

Reflective Practice

  • Examine preconceptions about Indigenous peoples.
  • Understand the influence of personal values in professional settings.
  • Commit to unlearning biases through critical, social reflection.

Core Questions for Reflection

  • What is the purpose of our service and whom does it serve?
  • What does cultural safety mean here?
  • How do we ensure equity and prevent assimilation?

Minimising Power Differentials

Use your position to empower Indigenous voices and avoid imposing Western norms inappropriately. Honour cultural difference over uniformity.

Structural and Systemic Considerations

  • Address power imbalances and structural violence.
  • Create clear pathways to empowerment and participation.

Indigenous Research

  • Embed Indigenous leadership and methodologies in all research projects.
  • Ensure ethical safeguards for community involvement and data sovereignty.

Community Engagement

  • Maintain authentic partnerships with local Indigenous communities.
  • Embed cultural protocols and create visible spaces of respect.

Acknowledgement to Country

We acknowledge the Traditional Custodians of the lands across all La Trobe campuses and pay respect to Elders past, present and emerging.

Resistance & History

Explore key resistance movements, from Pemulwuy’s War to the Uluru Statement from the Heart, to contextualise the impact of colonisation.
